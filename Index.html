About This Simulator

This project is a geopolitical decision-making simulator inspired by the complex dynamics surrounding Taiwan Strait tensions in 2024. The simulator models how various factors, such as the balance of power, regime stability, and external pressures, may influence key decisions by political leaders, specifically within the context of China's leadership.

Key Features

  • Interactive Controls: Five adjustable sliders represent critical factors in decision-making:
    • US-China Power Balance: Measures the perceived relative strength of the United States and China.
    • Regime Stability: Reflects the internal stability of the Chinese government.
    • Nationalism Pressure: Captures public and political sentiment pushing for aggressive actions.
    • Information Distortion: Accounts for the impact of propaganda and misinformation within the regime.
    • Escalation Control: Represents the leadership's ability to prevent conflict escalation.
  • Randomization Factors: Simulates unpredictable elements in decision-making through dynamic adjustment of paranoia and distortion levels, reflecting biases and incomplete information.
  • Outcome Calculation: Combines all adjusted factors into a final "Likelihood of War" score. This score predicts whether a leader would consider war, hesitate, or avoid conflict.

How It Works

When you adjust the sliders, each factor is recalculated using a unique algorithm. These inputs are modified by random factors (representing paranoia and distortion) to simulate subjective decision-making rather than objective reality. The likelihood score is then computed using weighted contributions from each factor:

  • 30%: US-China Power Balance
  • 20%: Regime Stability
  • 30%: Nationalism Pressure
  • 10%: Information Distortion
  • 10%: Escalation Control

The final score is clamped between 1 and 10 for clarity. Based on the score:

  • 1-4: "Risk too high, no action."
  • 5-7: "Hesitation and continued observation."
  • 8-10: "High likelihood of military action."

Why This Matters

This project aims to provide an educational and interactive way to understand the complexities of political decision-making under uncertainty. By visualizing how small changes in one factor (e.g., public pressure) can dramatically alter outcomes, users gain insights into the delicate balance of power and stability in global geopolitics.

This project models decision-making in a scenario where the risk of war is assessed based on key geopolitical factors. Each slider allows you to adjust a specific factor, which contributes to a final score predicting the "likelihood of war."

How It Works

The simulator uses the following steps to calculate the outcome:

  1. Input: Adjust sliders to modify values for power balance, regime stability, nationalism, information distortion, and escalation control.
  2. Randomization: Introduces small, unpredictable variations in "paranoia" and "distortion," simulating subjective biases or misinterpretations.
  3. Weighted Calculation: Combines the slider values into a single "Likelihood of War" score. Each factor is weighted as follows:
    • 30% from US-China Power Balance
    • 20% from Regime Stability
    • 30% from Nationalism Pressure
    • 10% from Information Distortion
    • 10% from Escalation Control
  4. Outcome: The score is presented on a scale of 1 to 10:
    • 1-4: Conflict is "unlikely." Risk too high.
    • 5-7: "Hesitation." Decision-making remains cautious.
    • 8-10: "High Likelihood of War." Aggressive action considered likely.

Purpose

This simulator highlights the complexity of geopolitical decision-making. By experimenting with different factors, you can explore how small changes in one area (e.g., regime stability) might lead to drastically different outcomes. It’s a tool to better understand the delicate balance of power, public pressure, and leadership biases in global politics.
